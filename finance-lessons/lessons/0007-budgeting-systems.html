Lesson 7 · Planning

Budgeting systems: a plan, not just a record

Continues Lesson 6 · ~9 minutes · grounded in Audit, missing features

Hoalu records what you spent. A budget decides what you will spend — before you spend it. The difference is the difference between a rear-view mirror and a steering wheel. A finance app without budgets can only describe the past; it cannot help you drive the future.

Three systems, one choice

Definition

50/30/20
A simple proportion rule: ~50% needs, ~30% wants, ~20% savings/debt. Easiest to start; coarsest in control.
Zero-based (envelope)
Give every unit of income a job before you spend it. Allocate all income into envelopes (categories + savings) so income − allocations = 0. Tightest control; most work.
Category budgets
Set a monthly cap per category (Food 6,000,000 ₫, Transport 2,000,000 ₫…) and track actual vs cap. The middle ground most apps use.
SystemEffortControlBest for
50/30/20LowLowBeginners, stable income, no debt trouble
Category budgetsMediumMediumMost households — flexibility with guardrails
Zero-basedHighHighTight cash flow, debt payoff, variable income
The core idea they share. Every system is a plan (a cap or allocation) compared against an actual. The budget is the plan; the expenses are the actual. Without the plan, you only have the actual — which is what Hoalu has today.

The number a budget lives or dies on: budget vs actual

The whole point of a budget is the comparison. For each category, each month:

remaining = budget cap − spent so far

That "remaining" is the steering wheel. Mid-month, Food has 1,500,000 ₫ left against a 6,000,000 ₫ cap? You can pace yourself. Already at 5,800,000 ₫ by the 10th? Pull back. Without the cap, you only learn you overspent after the month closes — too late to steer.

Rollover: why a month is not an island

Real spending is lumpy. You buy a year of insurance in March; you skip groceries in April while travelling. A rigid monthly cap punishes the lump; a rollover lets underspend carry forward as next month's buffer, and overspend carry forward as a debt against next month's cap. Rollover is what makes category budgets survive real life — and it is also exactly the mechanic behind sinking funds (Lesson 10).

In Hoalu — only event budgets exist

  • The only budget field in the entire schema is on event — a per-event cap, not a recurring monthly cap per category. (Audit, missing features)
  • There is no budget-vs-actual reporting outside events — no "Food: 4,200,000 of 6,000,000" card.
  • No rollover, no envelopes, no allocation. The 50/30/20 and zero-based systems cannot be expressed at all.

The smallest useful version: a budget per (workspace, category, month) with a cap and a spent-so-far rollup — then a dashboard card of remaining per category. That alone turns Hoalu from a record into a plan.

The win

Pick a system for your household this month — start with 50/30/20 if you've never budgeted, category budgets if you want control. Write one cap per category on paper before the month begins. At the end, compare actual to cap. The gap between plan and reality is where every useful financial insight lives — and it is the gap Hoalu must learn to show.
