Lesson 4 · The balance-sheet half

Credit cards are debt instruments, not wallets

Continues Lesson 3 · ~8 minutes · grounded in Audit §2

A credit card is the one account Hoalu most badly misunderstands. Treated as a wallet, it looks like any other container of money. Treated correctly, it is a revolving loan with a cycle, a grace period, and a punishment for carrying a balance. Get this right and the whole "available credit vs balance owed" picture opens up.

The five numbers a credit card carries

Definition

Credit limit
The most the bank will let you owe at once. A stock — fixed by the bank.
Balance owed
What you currently owe. A stock that rises with each purchase and falls with each payment.
Available credit
Limit − Balance owed. How much more you can spend right now.
Statement balance
What you owed on the statement closing date. Paying this in full avoids interest.
APR
Annual interest rate applied to any balance carried past the due date.

The cycle, and the one rule that makes credit free

Every card runs a monthly billing cycle with two dates that matter:

DateWhat happens
Statement closing dateThe bank totals your month's purchases → the statement balance.
Payment due date~25 days later. Pay the statement balance in full by this date → no interest.

The window between those two dates is the grace period — the bank's money you are using for free. The entire game of "using credit cards well" is: spend on the card for rewards and fraud protection, then pay the statement balance in full by the due date, every month, forever. Do that and the APR is irrelevant. Slip once and interest charges dating back to each purchase erase months of rewards.

Transactor vs revolnver. A transactor pays in full each month — pays no interest, uses the bank's money free. A revolver carries a balance — pays interest at rates that often exceed 20% APR. The app must show which mode the user is in, because a revolver's credit card is a liability on the balance sheet; a transactor's, paid in full, is essentially zero liability.

Why "available credit" is the number you watch

For spending decisions, available credit (limit − owed) is the live constraint, not the limit. A card with a 50,000,000 ₫ limit and 42,000,000 ₫ owed has only 8,000,000 ₫ of headroom — and a 84% utilisation that will hurt the user's credit profile. Hoalu must show available credit and utilisation, not just "a wallet named Visa."

In Hoalu — the card is an empty label

wallet.type can be "credit-card", but the table has none of the five numbers above — no limit, no balance owed, no statement date, no due date, no APR. (Audit §2) So the app:

  • Cannot tell you your available credit before a purchase.
  • Cannot warn you when utilisation is high.
  • Cannot show the card as a liability on the balance sheet (Lesson 2).
  • Cannot model the grace period or flag a balance being carried (the expensive case).

A credit card in Hoalu today is a name with a currency and an emoji. Financially it carries no information at all.

The win

For each card you hold, write down today: limit, balance owed, available credit, statement date, due date, APR. Then mark whether you pay in full (transactor) or carry a balance (revolver). That single page is what Hoalu must eventually reproduce per card — and it is also the page that tells you, right now, whether your cards are free money or expensive debt.
