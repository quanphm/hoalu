Lesson 5 · The cash-flow half

Cash flow & the savings rate: the engine, measured

Continues Lesson 4 · ~8 minutes · grounded in Audit, missing features

Lesson 2 said the balance sheet is the scoreboard and cash flow is the engine. Hoalu already tracks the engine — income and expenses — but never computes the one number that tells you whether the engine is actually driving you forward: the savings rate.

Definition

Cash flow
Income − Expenses over a period. Positive means you spent less than you earned; negative means you went backwards.
Savings
The positive part of cash flow — money left after spending. ("Savings" here means not spent, whether it sits in cash, pays debt, or buys investments.)
Savings rate
Savings ÷ Income × 100, usually over a month or year. The percentage of income you kept.

Why the savings rate is the single most predictive number

Income alone tells you nothing — a person earning 100,000,000 ₫ and spending 100,000,000 ₫ has a 0% savings rate and zero progress. A person earning 30,000,000 ₫ and spending 20,000,000 ₫ has a 33% rate and is quietly getting richer every month. The savings rate, not the income, sets your trajectory toward financial independence (we will quantify this in Lesson 13). A small rate sustained beats a large rate that collapses.

Savings is broader than "cash in the bank." Money used to pay down a credit-card balance above the minimum, or to buy investments, is savings — it grew your net worth. The rate measures money not consumed, wherever it lands.

A worked month

Item₫
Salary (income)30,000,000
Side income4,000,000
Total income34,000,000
Total expenses25,000,000
Savings (cash flow)9,000,000

Savings rate = 9,000,000 ÷ 34,000,000 × 100 ≈ 26%

A 26% rate is solid. A commonly cited healthy target is 20%+; financial-independence seekers aim for 50% and above. The number itself is less important than that it is measured, every month, and trending up.

In Hoalu — the engine is tracked, the output is not

Hoalu records income and expenses (the inputs) and even shows period-over-period % on the dashboard — but it never computes savings or the savings rate. The dashboard's three stat cards are Incomes / Expenses / Transactions; the missing card is Savings. (Audit, missing features)

This is the cheapest, highest-value addition to the app: one subtraction (income − expense) and one division (÷ income) surface the number that predicts everything else. No new tables, no new entities — just arithmetic on data Hoalu already has.

The win

For last month, total your income and your expenses (Hoalu already holds both). Compute the savings rate. Write it down. Next month, do it again. The trend of that single percentage is the truest measure of whether your household's money management is improving — far more honest than the income number alone.
