Lesson 9 · Operations

Multi-currency done right: rate at the transaction date

Continues Lesson 8 · ~9 minutes · grounded in Audit §5–8, §12

Your family may earn in one currency and spend in another. That makes the app's job genuinely hard — and Hoalu's FX pipeline is where the most silent data corruption lives. Every defect here quietly misstates your totals, with no warning.

The one principle

Definition

Rate-at-transaction-date
Convert a foreign amount using the exchange rate that was valid on the transaction's own date — never the date you entered it, and never a single rate for a whole month.
Reporting currency
The single currency in which all totals are expressed for comparison. Should be a user setting, not a heuristic.

Why the transaction date? An expense on 1 March and one on 31 March can sit at very different rates, especially for volatile currencies. A single month-end rate makes the 1st look as if it happened at month-end prices — a small lie per transaction, a large lie in the sum. The transaction date is the only honest anchor.

Float cross-rate maths Audit §12parseFloat drift in X→USD→YUse decimal/integer minor-units
Cross-rates. To convert VND→EUR you usually go VND→USD→EUR because direct VND/EUR rates are rare. Each hop must use the rate valid on the transaction date. Hoalu's cross-rate is right in shape; it just loses precision by doing it in floating point and at the wrong date.

The cardinal sin: silent zeroing

Of all the FX defects, the worst is silently dropping an amount when no rate is found. A user with a USD expense in a VND workspace, no rate loaded, sees that expense contribute nothing to every total — with no warning. Their monthly spend looks lower than it is; their savings rate looks higher; their budget looks under. They make decisions on numbers that are quietly, invisibly wrong.

Principle

A missing rate is a data problem the user must be told about, not a reason to set the amount to zero. Surface it: "3 expenses can't be totalled — update the USD→VND rate for these dates." Never let a missing input silently corrupt an output.

In Hoalu — five defects, one pipeline

The FX pipeline is a single chain, so each defect compounds: a backdated March expense with no rate, summed at month-end in float, in a workspace whose reporting currency was guessed by count — that one expense is wrong five ways at once, and the dashboard shows a confident-looking total that is fiction. (Audit §5–8, §12)

The fix is a decision, not a refactor: one principle (rate at txn date), one setting (reporting currency), one rule (missing rate = visible error). Apply those three and the pipeline becomes honest.

The win

If your household uses more than one currency, write down which. Choose your reporting currency (the one you think in). For your last foreign expense, find the exchange rate on the actual transaction date — not today's. The discipline of "what was the rate on the day it happened?" is the whole habit; the app should enforce it, not you.
