Lesson 3 · The balance-sheet half

Accounts done right: one wallet is not enough

Continues Lesson 2 · ~8 minutes · grounded in Audit §1–2

A wallet is not just a name and a currency. Each kind of account has a job in the household system, and each carries different financial data. Hoalu currently treats them all as interchangeable labels — that is the root of the credit-card and balance problems.

The five accounts a household actually needs

Source. This architecture is Ramit Sethi's "conscious spending" system from I Will Teach You to Be Rich — checking, savings, credit card, investment, and (for debt) a separate payoff target. It is the cleanest practical layout.
AccountJobWhat it must carry
CheckingDaily operating account — paycheque in, bills out.Opening balance, current balance
SavingsReserve & sinking funds — money parked for future use.Opening balance, current balance, goal allocations
Credit cardShort-term borrowing, paid off monthly for rewards.Credit limit, balance owed, available credit, statement date, due date, APR
InvestmentLong-term growth — net-worth engine, not spending money.Holdings, current value (net worth only)
Loan / mortgageLong-term debt to pay down.Principal owed, interest rate, term, monthly payment

Notice the third column: only checking and savings share the same shape (opening + current balance). A credit card is not "a wallet with a negative balance" — it has a limit, a statement cycle, a due date, and an APR that a plain balance cannot represent. A loan has an amortisation schedule. An investment account has holdings whose value floats daily. One wallet table with a type enum cannot hold all of this, and that is why Hoalu's model breaks down at the credit card.

Opening balance: the number that makes everything reconcile

Every account that holds money needs an opening balance — the amount in it at the moment you start tracking. Without it, "current balance" is undefined. With it, the rule is simple:

The reconciliation identity

current balance = opening balance + Σ(income) − Σ(expenses) − Σ(transfers out) + Σ(transfers in)

Every transaction that touches the account must move its balance. If the app has no opening balance, the sum starts at zero and every "balance" you see is fiction. This single missing field is why Hoalu cannot show how much money anyone has.

In Hoalu — five types, one shape, no balance

  • wallet.type has the right five labels (cash, bank-account, credit-card, debit-card, digital-account) — but every type uses the same columns: name, description, currency, isActive. No balance. No credit limit. No APR. No due date. (Audit §1, §2)
  • The wallet table shows a row count where a balance sum should be. (Audit §1)
  • Loans and investment accounts have no entity at all — they cannot exist in the app.

The fix is not code; it is a decision: give each account type the financial fields its job requires, and make opening balance non-negotiable on every cash-holding account.

The win

List your real accounts on paper, one per line, with the column each needs. For each, write the opening balance you would enter today. You will immediately see which accounts Hoalu can already hold (checking, savings — if it had a balance field) and which it structurally cannot (credit card, loan, investment). That map is the spec for the balance-sheet half of the app.
