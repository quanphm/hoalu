Money Management for Hoalu

A 13-lesson course: learn personal & family finance, then audit and improve the Hoalu app. Each lesson is short, has an interactive drill, and ties to a concrete gap in the app. Start at 1, or jump to any.

Arc 1 — Foundations: the half Hoalu is blind to

  1. Stock vs. Flow
    The one distinction that explains why Hoalu feels like a spending diary. The app tracks flows; it has no stocks.
  2. The Balance Sheet
    Assets, liabilities, net worth — the scoreboard Hoalu cannot show.
  3. Accounts done right
    The five account types, the data each must carry, and why opening balance is non-negotiable.
  4. Credit cards as debt
    Limit, balance owed, available credit, grace period, APR — the card is a loan, not a wallet.

Arc 2 — The cash-flow half: the engine, measured

  1. Cash flow & the savings rate
    The single most predictive number — and the cheapest, highest-value card Hoalu is missing.
  2. Transfers
    Why moving your own money is neither income nor expense, and why fake transfers corrupt the numbers.
  3. Budgeting systems
    50/30/20 vs. category budgets vs. zero-based. A plan, not just a record.

Arc 3 — Operations: getting the plumbing right

  1. Recurring bills done right
    Hoalu's strongest feature — plus the dueDay=0 bug, the subscription audit, and silent behaviour as a sin.
  2. Multi-currency done right
    Rate at the transaction date, and the five FX defects that silently corrupt Hoalu's totals.
  3. Savings goals & sinking funds
    Turn known future lumps into smooth monthly contributions; the future gets an address.

Arc 4 — The long game: building net worth over time

  1. Debt & loans
    Amortization, the minimum-payment trap, and avalanche vs. snowball.
  2. Investing basics
    Inflation, compounding, the three-fund portfolio — the growth line the balance sheet needs.
  3. Financial independence & the 4% rule
    The capstone: FI number, the 4% rule, and how the savings rate sets the whole timeline.
