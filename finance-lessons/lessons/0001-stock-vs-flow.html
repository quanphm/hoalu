Lesson 1 · Foundations

Stock vs. Flow: the one distinction your app is half-blind to

Grounded in the Hoalu Financial Audit · ~8 minutes · tied to your mission

Every number in personal finance is one of two kinds. Learn to tell them apart and you will suddenly see why Hoalu feels incomplete — and what every real finance app must have that Hoalu does not.

Definition

Stock
A quantity measured at a single point in time. "How much right now?" — your bank balance today, your debt as of Dec 31, your net worth this moment.
Flow
A quantity measured over a period of time. "How much moved during March?" — your salary earned this month, your groceries spent this month, your savings rate this year.

The words come from physics, where the same split exists: the water in a bathtub is a stock (litres, now); the water flowing through the tap is a flow (litres per minute). A bathtub without a water level is useless — and so is a finance app that only counts the tap.

The two statements this maps to

Accountants formalise this split into two documents. You do not need to be an accountant, but you must know which one is which:

Balance SheetCash-Flow Statement
KindStocksFlows
QuestionWhere do I stand right now?What moved this month?
ContainsAssets, liabilities → net worthIncome, expenses → savings
Snapshot?Yes — a single dateNo — a date range

Here is the crucial relationship: flows change stocks. Earn income (a flow in) and your bank balance (a stock) rises. Spend on groceries (a flow out) and it falls. The cash-flow statement is the engine; the balance sheet is the scoreboard. A household that only watches the engine and never the scoreboard cannot tell whether it is actually getting richer.

In Hoalu — the half that is missing

Hoalu today is almost entirely a cash-flow app: it records expenses and income (flows) and charts them over periods. What it lacks is the balance sheet:

  • No wallet balances — the wallet table has no balance column at all, so the app cannot say "you have X VND right now." (Audit §1)
  • No net worth — no assets-vs-liabilities view, no net-worth-over-time series. (Audit, missing-features table)
  • The dashboard "balance" is not a balance — the cash-flow chart starts from zero each period, so it shows period net flow dressed up as an account balance. Change the date range and the "balance" jumps, because nothing is carried forward. (Audit §3)

That is the entire reason the app feels like a spending diary rather than a money manager: it counts the tap, never the water level.

The one win from this lesson

Look at any feature in any finance app and ask one question: is this number a stock or a flow? If a "balance" depends on which date range you picked, it is a flow pretending to be a stock — and that is a design smell. Real balances are point-in-time. Real flows are period-scoped. Hold that distinction and the rest of this course will slot into place.

Primary source to read next

Your Money or Your Life — Vicki Robin & Joe Dominguez. The whole method turns on tracking every penny (flows) so that you can see your net worth rise toward the "crossover point" (a stock). It is the clearest argument for why a finance app must hold both halves — and it is the philosophical parent of the balance-sheet feature Hoalu is missing.
